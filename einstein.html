Albert Einstein

About

  • Albert Einstein was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany
  • He is best known for developing the theories of special and general relativity, which revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and gravity
  • Einstein won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, which was crucial in the development of quantum theory
  • His mass-energy equivalence formula, (E=mc^2), is one of the most famous equations in the world
  • Einstein moved to the United States in 1933 and spent the rest of his life there, passing away on April 18, 1955, in Princeton, New Jersey

Most Famous Equations

  1. Mass-Energy Equivalence: E = mc²
  2. Einstein Field Equations of General Relativity:
    Rμν - (1/2) R gμν + Λ gμν = (8πG/c⁴) Tμν
  3. Photoelectric Effect Equation: E = hν - φ
  4. Einstein Relation in Brownian Motion: D = μkBT
  5. Bose-Einstein Statistics Formula:
    n(ε) = 1 / [e(ε - μ)/(kBT) - 1]
